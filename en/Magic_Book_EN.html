BOOK ONE · FUNDAMENTALS
Nature of Magic & Fundamental Theory
The Unified Theory of All Magic

In the world of Corruption, magic is not made of some mysterious external element, nor a privilege granted by the gods to a chosen few. It is part of the world's own inner logic — a fundamental capacity present within every living being that possesses a soul.

The nature of magic is "the soul and consciousness interfering with reality."
— Hybrid System · Unified Foundational Definition

Every act of spellcasting arises from the same mechanism: some form of mental activity within the caster exerts an influence on the laws governing external reality. Different casting traditions and different cultural understandings are simply different ways of describing and channeling this same process — not different sources of power.

The Five Foundational Elements of Spellcasting
ElementDefinitionEffect of Its Loss
ConsciousnessThe impulse that initiates interference with reality. The starting point of all magic.Cannot cast; perception tends toward shutdown
Soul StructureStabilizes the casting process, preventing the interference of consciousness from spiraling out of control.Spell outcomes become unpredictable, may harm the caster
Cognitive ModelThe caster's own framework for understanding "how a spell is supposed to work."The spell may take effect in unexpected ways
Mana FlowThe force of interference with reality generated by the soul's activity — the fuel of magic.The spell's effect fades until it disappears entirely
Channeling MethodThe path by which reality is made to accept the consciousness's interference (language, ritual, faith, etc.).The interference has nothing to land on, like shouting into a void
✦
The True Nature of Mana
Mana is not an external element, not something drawn up from the earth or sky. It is the force of reality-interference generated by the soul's own activity — closer to:

· Mental fuel · The flow of will · The soul's own heat

This means the true cost of casting is closer in nature to mental fatigue than to physical exertion. A caster after heavy spellcasting resembles someone whose nerves have been strained to exhaustion, rather than someone with sore muscles.
Practical manifestations: heaviness in the head, sluggish thinking, difficulty concentrating, emotional numbness. High-level casters typically have extensive experience reading these signs and know when it's time to stop.

For this reason, when writing about magic in this world, always keep in mind: magic and a caster's mental state are inseparable. Someone in a state of extreme emotional instability will struggle to cast with precision; someone in a state of intense focus, even nearly out of mana, may still manage a delicate, minor spell.

BOOK ONE · ADDENDUM
Unclassified Phenomena
Phenomena Beyond the Known Framework

The following phenomena have been recorded by the casting community, but cannot be placed into any of the six known schools. They do not follow the established theoretical framework of magic, nor do they display any lasting regularity.

  • 01 A streetlamp on a certain street changes color on rainy days, the color linked somehow to the mood of the first passerby of the day. It reverts after three hours. Cannot be reproduced on demand.
  • 02 A black cat that always appears under the same eave. Some believe it to be a sealed object, but no detection method has ever confirmed any magical residue on it.
  • 03 Certain sealed objects, after being restored, will "revert" to their pre-restoration state after some indeterminate span of time, as if the restoration had never happened — down to the restorer's own marks reappearing exactly as before.
  • 04 A very small number of people, in deep sleep, will speak a sentence in no known language. They remember nothing of it upon waking. These sentences have been recorded, but cannot be translated, and cannot be linked to any known instance of Corruption or any sealed object.
This volume does not attempt to explain these phenomena. They are recorded here only as a reminder: the magic system is a framework for human understanding of the world, not the whole of the world itself. There will always be things that fall outside the frame. — Editorial Note

BOOK TWO · THE COST SYSTEM
The Cost System of Magic
What It Takes to Shape Reality

All magic carries a cost, but in this world, that cost isn't blood and pain — it's something more restrained, more mundane: attention, energy, mental clarity. This gives the cost itself a lived-in, everyday quality — after casting, one needs a good night's sleep, or a while sitting quietly doing nothing, rather than collapsing to the ground, bleeding.

Mental Drain
The primary form of casting cost in this world, functioning much like a "mana-bar" style of mental expenditure — it doesn't directly reduce physical stamina, but drains the clarity and stability of consciousness.
Common manifestations: fatigue, reduced attention, mild headache, emotional numbness, slight sensory overload, sluggish thinking.
Ways to recover: sleep, rest, ordinary conversation with others, familiar surroundings, simple physical labor.
✦

If a caster continues high-intensity spellcasting before mental drain has recovered, they enter an overload state. The following are recorded manifestations of overload:

Overload State · 01
Soul Fatigue
Prolonged insomnia, difficulty entering deep rest. Casters describe it as "my mind won't stop," yet nothing in it comes into focus.
Overload State · 02
Sensory Distortion
Hearing or vision develops a slight offset — sound as if heard underwater, colors slightly off, edges blurred. Usually resolves on its own within a few hours.
Overload State · 03
Reality Lag
A sense that the outside world is running half a beat slow, and one's own reactions have slowed to match. Sometimes accompanied by a strange sense of "watching from outside," as if observing one's own body move.
Overload State · 04
Emotional Hollowness
A temporary loss of the capacity for emotional fluctuation — no reaction to things that would normally stir feeling. Not calm, but a kind of blankness. Usually lasts a few hours to a day.
Overload State · 05
Mana Tremor
Unconscious, random leakage of small-scale magical effects, beyond the caster's control. May cause nearby objects to shift slightly, temperature to fluctuate, or light to flicker.
Overload State · 06 (Extremely Rare)
Soul Structure Instability
Appears only in extremely high-level casters after prolonged overload. Cracks begin to form in the mental structure, and sensitivity to Corruption rises sharply. A boundary that demands particular attention.
Sample Scene · The Everyday Face of Cost
He'd spent four hours working on that sealed object. When he finished, Riku Yuni put his tools back and sat in the chair for a long while, doing nothing.
Not thinking about anything in particular — just sitting.
The other persona kept him quiet company, without a word. That kind of moment doesn't need words.
The rain was still falling outside. The tea had gone cold, and no one bothered to reheat it.
Things to Avoid When Writing This
· Mental breakdowns or personality splits at the drop of a hat
· Overload immediately turning into a frenzied killing spree
· Costs wildly out of proportion to the spell's intensity, played for melodrama
This world's overall tone leans toward the everyday. Overload should feel more like "needs a good night's sleep" than "on the verge of a soul explosion." Please keep that temperature.

BOOK THREE · FAITH ARTS
Faith Arts
Magic Through Belief and Divine Resonance
FAITH ARTS
Faith Arts
Magic of belief, prayer and spiritual resonance
Most Stable · Safest
Core Mechanism: through a stable state of faith and the act of prayer, the caster's consciousness is anchored within a highly stable cognitive framework, reducing uncertainty during casting and allowing the interference with reality to be smoother and longer-lasting. Whether the god itself actually exists has no bearing on whether the spell works — what matters is the mental stability that faith itself provides.
Common Uses
Healing minor wounds and illness
Purifying low-level anomalous residue
Stabilizing another's emotional state
Dispelling low-level anomalous interference
Maintaining long-term seal stability
Comfort and soul-stabilization for the dying
Limitations
Low ceiling on extreme power
Heavily dependent on the caster's own conviction
Effectiveness plummets when the caster harbors doubt
Weak interference at the level of the soul
Heavily ritualistic, ill-suited to emergency casting
Typical Practitioners
Clergy and monastics
Folk believers (low level)
Church-certified healers
Relationship to Other Schools
Combined with Word Arts: reinforces seal stability
Combined with Ritual Arts: the mainstream method for major Church ceremonies
In tension with Soul Arts: faith's closed nature limits soul perception
Typical Casting Phenomena · What You'd Observe
  • The environment gradually grows quiet
  • Light softens
  • Air temperature rises slightly
  • Emotions of those present are gently stabilized
  • A low murmur of prayer, or silence
Faith Arts Are Not "Holy Light Explosions"
The hallmark of advanced Faith Arts isn't a dazzling light show, but a sense that "the whole environment has quietly become different." Holy light does exist, but it tends to manifest as a faint, gentle glow, like early morning sunlight — never a screen full of pyrotechnics.
Reference imagery: candlelight suddenly holding perfectly steady · the rain seeming to pause for an instant · dust in the room ceasing to drift

BOOK FOUR · WORD ARTS
Word Arts
Magic Through Language, Name, and Definition
WORD ARTS
A thing correctly described is more easily accepted by reality.
— Word Arts · Foundational Belief

Word Arts rests on a core belief: language is not merely a tool for describing reality — it is itself a means of affecting reality. When you name a thing, or define a state, with sufficiently precise language, you are, in some sense, reshaping reality's own "understanding" of that thing, making it easier to change.

Word Arts
Magic through precise language, inscription and naming
Most Precise · Academic
Core Mechanism: through language, script, name, and definition, alters reality's own "cognitive framework" for the target object, allowing change to be exerted within that framework. The more precise the language, the finer the interference; the more complete the naming, the more stable the control.
Common Uses
Sealing and unsealing
Repairing an object's structure
Precise record-keeping and archiving
Constructing magical arrays
Stabilizing a sealed object's resonance
Transmitting information across distance
Limitations
Extremely long learning curve
Requires deep focus; ill-suited to casting under emotional strain
A misplaced word can cause the spell to go awry
Limited effect on things that "cannot be named"
Typical Practitioners
Scholars and researchers
Scribes and archivists
Sealed-object restorers (one of their core schools)
Relationship to Other Schools
Combined with Faith Arts: precision sealing
Combined with Soul Arts: advanced sealed-object restoration
Combined with Alchemy: fixing incantations into material
Typical Casting Phenomena · What You'd Observe
  • Text briefly flickers in the air as an afterimage
  • The caster murmurs a single word, repeatedly, under their breath
  • Paper automatically records content
  • A named object gives off a faint resonance
  • A regular pattern of lines appears along a sealed object's edge
When Word Arts fails, it usually isn't an explosion — it's nothing happening at all, or a minor, mild mishap.

Word Arts practitioners tend to be men and women of few words in daily life, but every word they use is extremely precise. They have a natural discomfort with vague expression — not merely a professional habit, but a sensitivity that runs down to the level of consciousness itself. They tend to "name a thing clearly" in their own minds before ever saying it aloud.

BOOK FIVE · SOUL ARTS
Soul Arts
Direct Contact with Consciousness and Memory
SOUL ARTS

Of all the casting traditions, Soul Arts sits closest to the true underlying principle of magic — it operates directly on consciousness and soul structure themselves, rather than through an intermediary such as language, faith, or ritual. This makes it both the most powerful and the most dangerous of all the schools.

Soul Arts
Direct manipulation of consciousness, memory and soul structure
Most Sensitive · Extremely High Risk
Core Mechanism: the caster makes direct contact, through their own consciousness, with the target's soul, establishing a direct channel of perception and interference between the two minds. The effect is precise, but the boundary is blurred — it's difficult for a caster to guarantee they won't be affected, in turn, by the target's own soul state during contact.
Common Uses
Mental communication
Memory reading (within limits)
Emotional soothing and stabilization
Dream contact
Soul-structure repair
Entering another's mental world (advanced, extremely dangerous)
Limitations
Extremely high mental load; highest overload risk of any school
Long-term use leads to emotional numbness
Practitioners have the highest sensitivity to Corruption of any school
Cannot effectively act on an "unconscious" existence
Typical Practitioners
A small number of high-level soul healers
Some sealed-object restorers
Riku Yuni (naturally suited; the dual-persona structure provides a unique dimension of perception)
Relationship to Corruption
Soul Arts operate on a mechanism closest to that of Corruption itself
Soul Arts practitioners are the type of host Corruption finds most interesting
Entering and rescuing someone from a mental world requires this school
Riku Yuni's Special Relationship to Soul Arts
Riku Yuni's parallel dual-persona structure grants him, at the level of Soul Arts, an exceedingly rare trait: he can perceive another's soul state from two conscious vantage points simultaneously, something like "dual-channel reception." This lets him read a sealed object's resonance with far more nuance than an ordinary restorer — but it also means he carries a heavier mental load.
Note: this doesn't make him "the strongest Soul Arts practitioner." He simply perceives things in a way no one else can — and that perception is, at times, one he'd rather not have.
Typical Casting Phenomena · What You'd Observe
  • The caster's eyes take on a look of "not being here"
  • The other party may slip into a light trance
  • The surrounding air cools slightly
  • The caster needs a long silence afterward
  • Sometimes cannot immediately articulate what they saw

BOOK SIX · ELEMENT ARTS
Element Arts
Resonance With the Natural World
ELEMENT ARTS
Element Arts is not "creating an element" — it's "establishing resonance with a natural phenomenon."
— Element Arts · Correcting a Core Misconception

Element Arts is the most widely practiced and lowest-threshold casting tradition in this world. Most of the basic magic ordinary people know comes from this school — not because it's simple, but because its channeling path is the most intuitive: you don't need to understand a complex soul structure, only to form a genuine, felt sense of resonance with a natural phenomenon.

Element Arts
Resonance with natural phenomena — fire, water, wind, earth
Most Intuitive · Most Common
Core Mechanism: the caster "personifies" a natural phenomenon (heat, flow, gravity, vibration, etc.) — that is, forms a relationship with it at the level of consciousness resembling communication — and then exerts influence on that phenomenon through the relationship. The deeper the resonance, the greater the range and precision of what can be affected.
Common Uses
Igniting fire · controlling heat
Adjusting the direction of water flow
Warming food and surroundings
Windproofing and insulation
Aiding agricultural irrigation
Structural reinforcement
Limitations
Extremely difficult to achieve fine control at high levels
Strongly dependent on current environmental conditions
No interference capability at the soul level
Prone to losing control under strong emotion
Typical Practitioners
Farmers · fishermen · herders
Cooks (precise heat control)
Construction workers
Most ordinary citizens (low level)
Typical Everyday Scenes
Lighting a hearth with a passing gesture
Keeping tea warm with a fingertip
Drying laundry on the line faster
Keeping a particular spot dry in the rain
Sample Scene · The Everyday Feel of Element Arts
The baker at the market rested a hand by the oven and closed her eyes for a moment. The oven's temperature settled — not too high, not too low, just right.
She said nothing special, struck no particular pose. She simply felt it, and adjusted.
It's the first thing she does every morning, even before her prayers.

BOOK SEVEN · RITUAL ARTS
Ritual Arts
Amplification Through Process and Collective Will
RITUAL ARTS

The core logic of Ritual Arts is "amplification," not "creation." A single caster's power of interference is limited, but through a carefully designed process, a system of symbols, and the convergence of collective will, that power can be multiplied many times over. This makes Ritual Arts the single most powerful form of casting, in terms of raw output per use.

Ritual Arts
Amplification through structured process and collective consciousness
Most Powerful · Most Complex
Core Mechanism: through a pre-designed process (steps, timing, symbolic objects, formation) the consciousness of all participants is directed toward the same goal, forming a combined force. The process itself becomes a vessel for guiding consciousness, reducing the instability of individual casting.
Common Uses
Constructing and maintaining large-scale seals
City-level protective works
Major Church ceremonies
Rebirth prayers and soul settlement
Seasonal agricultural rites
Magical witnessing of major vows
Limitations
Time-consuming preparation; unsuited for emergencies
Usually requires multiple participants
Any single step disrupted causes the whole process to fail
The cost of failure often far exceeds that of ordinary casting
Typical Practitioners
Professional ritual officiants
High-ranking Church clergy
Leaders of large-scale sealing projects
Folk Forms
Collective prayer during festivals
Magical witnessing at weddings and funerals
Village seasonal rites (low-level, mild effects)
The "Weight" of Ritual Arts
When a true high-level ritual begins, there is no explosion, no light show at the scene. It's closer to this: the very nature of the space itself changes. Sound travels differently, the angle of light seems subtly off, and those present instinctively lower their voices, sometimes forgetting what they meant to say.
If you ever write a scene where "everyone suddenly falls quiet, for no clear reason" — that's very likely a ritual underway.

BOOK EIGHT · ALCHEMY
Alchemy
Magic Fixed in Matter and Craft
ALCHEMY

Alchemy is the casting tradition most deeply woven into everyday life — not because its effects are the mildest, but because its products remain in the world in physical form, becoming objects people encounter every day. That teapot that remembers a certain temperature, that mirror that clouds over on rainy days, that ring that always seems to be somewhere you didn't leave it — all of these might well be products of Alchemy.

Alchemy
Inscription of magic into physical matter and craft objects
Most Durable · Most Artisanal
Core Mechanism: through a specific process, careful choice of materials, and the infusion of the caster's own consciousness, magical effects are fixed into a material structure. The object becomes a vessel for the magic, continuing to function long after the caster has left, or even died — sometimes developing a "personality" the original design never intended.
Common Uses
Everyday magical tool-making
Constructing sealing containers
Preserving potions and preparations
Materials for sealed-object restoration
Communication devices
Building reinforcement and embedded magic
Limitations
High cost, heavily material-dependent
Time-consuming to craft; unavailable for instant use
Final quality is highly dependent on the caster's state during creation
"Personality drift" from years of use is difficult to predict
Typical Practitioners
Workshop artisans and craftsmen
Sealed-object restorers (a core skill)
Professional potion-makers
Some architects (structural magic embedding)
Sealed Objects & Alchemy
Most sealed objects contain some element of Alchemy
Restoring a sealed object requires understanding its alchemical structure
One of the two core schools underlying Riku Yuni's daily work
Sample Scene · The Workshop Feel of Alchemy
Three kinds of material sat on the workbench, alongside a fine-tipped brush and a small vial of sealing fluid.
Riku Yuni didn't rush to begin — he circled the object once first, as if listening to it speak.
"This bit of incantation was added later," he said. "Whoever added it wasn't paying attention at the time."
He picked up the brush and began stripping that section away. The motion was slow, and steady.

BOOK NINE · COMBINED APPLICATIONS
Combined Applications & Interactions
What Happens When Schools Work Together

Casters in this world almost never rely on a single school alone. Specializing in one tradition doesn't mean ignorance of the others — more often, a high-level caster will naturally shift between traditions according to need, or deliberately combine several to produce new effects. This "chemical reaction" is often the most interesting part.

Faith Arts
+
Word Arts
Precision Seal Construction
Faith provides a stable foundation; Word Arts provides precise structure. A seal made this way resists erosion better than pure Word Arts alone, and is more finely controllable than pure Faith Arts alone.
The Church's standard method for official sealing.
Soul Arts
+
Alchemy
Memory Vessel
Seals a specific memory or emotional imprint into a physical container, allowing it to be read afterward by anyone with Soul Arts perception, even after the original caster is gone.
Existing memory vessels are extremely rare, mostly ancient. Riku Yuni occasionally comes across relics of this kind.
Element Arts
+
Ritual Arts
City Heating System
Amplifies elemental resonance through ritual, establishing an ongoing heat-distribution network across a city. Requires periodic maintenance rituals.
Some mid-sized towns rely on such a system through the winter; it's treated as part of the basic infrastructure.
Word Arts
+
Soul Arts
Advanced Sealed-Object Restoration
Word Arts provides the structural framework, Soul Arts provides direct communication with the object's resonance. Finer than restoration using either school alone, but the mental cost to the caster multiplies accordingly.
Riku Yuni's core working method — he uses this combination constantly.
Faith Arts
+
Ritual Arts
Major Church Ceremonies
The Church's most mainstream form of high-level casting. Collective faith, amplified through ritual structure, achieves a scale of effect no individual caster could ever reach alone.
City-level wards and historically significant seals are all achieved through this combination.
Alchemy
+
Word Arts
Incantation-Fixed Material
Writes a specific incantation structure directly into a material's physical structure, letting it maintain a given effect continuously, with no caster present.
The basis for sealing containers and magical tools of every kind — an essential skill for restorers.

BOOK TEN · SOCIAL TAXONOMY
Social Classification of Practitioners
How Mages Are Understood in the World

This world has none of the stereotypes that come with the word "mage." A caster's place in society is closer to that of any other professional — first a monastic, scholar, artisan, healer, or researcher, and only secondarily a caster. Magic is a professional tool, not an identity label.

Social Type Primary School(s) Social Role Typical Daily Work
Clergy Faith Arts Ritual Arts Within the Church hierarchy, responsible for preaching, healing, and officiating rituals. The most publicly trusted group of casters. Morning prayers, healing, maintaining Church seals, assisting folk rituals
Scholars Word Arts Soul Arts Focused on researching magical mechanisms and anomalous phenomena. Often cooperate with the Church while maintaining a degree of independence. Textual research, incantation analysis, building anomaly theory
Soul Healers Soul Arts Faith Arts Extremely rare, specializing in mental trauma, soul damage, and cases of spiritual infection. Professional reputation is sharply divided. Treating those with mental anomalies, dream therapy, soul-structure repair
Workshop Artisans Alchemy Element Arts Makers of magical tools and everyday goods. Socially stable, in constant demand, but rarely receive much attention. Crafting and repairing magical objects, taking custom commissions
Sealed-Object Restorers Word Arts Soul Arts Alchemy Faith Arts (basic) A high-risk, high-income independent profession, taken on a commission basis with no fixed employer. Low social need, high professional barrier to entry.
Riku Yuni's profession.		 Seal restoration, appraisal of anomalous relics, resonance handling, structural research
Ritual Officiants Ritual Arts Faith Arts Full-time specialists who take on large-scale rituals. Work infrequently, but each undertaking is large in scale, usually with a fixed team. Ritual design and officiating, team coordination, site preparation
Wandering Casters Mixed, varies by individual Freelance casters with no fixed professional identity. Regarded as a destabilizing element, but also an important source of intelligence about anomalies. No fixed routine — travels for work, occasionally brings back strange news
Job Classes That Don't Exist Here
This world has no such thing as a "fire mage," "ice mage," "healer," or "spellblade" — no job defined by a single magical attribute. A caster's identity is determined by their social function, not by a position on a skill tree.
Please avoid describing casters in this world using RPG-style job-class logic. They are, at heart, artisans, scholars, and healers of every stripe.

BOOK ELEVEN · WRITING STYLE GUIDE
General Writing Style
The Texture and Temperature of This World

The rules of the magic system matter, but what matters more is making this world feel, to the reader, like it truly exists. A world's sense of reality rarely comes from grand designs — it comes from countless small details that make a reader think, "yes, that's exactly how it would be."

Magic isn't a superpower, and it isn't for becoming stronger.
It is "the way the soul speaks with the world."
— Corruption Magic System · Final Summary
The World's Base Tone
Quiet Lived-in Workshop Air Old Ward Gently Mysterious Soulful Faintly Strange Medieval Everyday Life Slow Pace Rain and Lamplight Occasional Anomaly Grounded Identity Emotion-Driven Everyday Magic
Not a hot-blooded power fantasy · not bleak despair · not constant high-pressure horror · not a spectacle-driven showcase world.
Every Time You Write This World, Ask Yourself
· Is the magic in this scene "background noise," rather than the main event?
· Is there a small detail here that makes the world feel "alive" (a kettle that remembers a temperature / a streetlamp that glows on rainy nights / a broom that follows someone around)?
· Is the level of horror right? Is the anomaly "a bit strange," or does it tip straight into meltdown?
· Has Riku Yuni been written as "a protagonist perpetually suffering"? He's simply living quietly, in his own way, inside a strange world.
· Is this scene's pacing slow enough?
How to Make the World Feel Like "It's Been Running for Centuries"
· Every object has a history: the way that chair is worn down, the sound of that door's hinge, the grass growing up through the paving cracks on that street.
· Every profession has its unspoken rules: restorers know certain things don't need saying, because those things have become the very air of the trade.
· Every anomaly has a story: that mirror that hates rainy days must have a reason, even if no one remembers what it is anymore.
· People's attitude toward magic is one of habit, not wonder: lighting a lamp with magic carries the same emotional weight as striking a match by hand.
FINAL SUMMARY · The Core Idea of the Magic System
In the world of Corruption, magic is not for becoming stronger,
not for defeating anything, not for becoming anyone.
It is how people relate to the world around them.
