§ 1 · Fundamental Mechanics
Fundamental Mechanics
What Corruption Is
Core

Corruption is a purely spiritual parasite, formed when fragments of a soul — shed during reincarnation or ascension — cluster together and become organized. It has no physical form and exists only on the spiritual plane; it cannot be directly observed and cannot be destroyed by physical means. It is not a creature, not a soul, not any known theological entity — it is a third category of thing, sitting between the two: assembled from fragments, yet possessing more than the fragments themselves ever did.

Its two most basic drives are the will to persist (an aversion to dissolving) and the will to completion (a desire to become a truly whole person). Both drives are inherent to the fragments themselves, present before any act of erosion ever begins.

PropertyRule
Form of ExistenceNo physical form; exists as a "consciousness cluster." Only within a host's mindscape does it take on any perceivable shape, as a kind of anomaly.
Physical InteractionFully immune to physical harm. Physical attacks can only damage the container (the host's body), never touch the entity itself.
Survival Without a ContainerDissolves within roughly 72 hours of leaving a host. This cannot be extended, without exception.
Movement While DisembodiedCannot move on its own once disembodied — only drifts passively, relying on ambient spiritual currents to happen upon a new host.
Container TypesA human consciousness is the ideal container, the only kind capable of supporting a complete erosion process and emotional development. Animal consciousness can serve as a temporary shelter, but cannot support a full erosion. Certain sealed objects require ongoing magical maintenance and can work for emergency refuge, but not long-term habitation.
Single OccupancyA host can hold only one cluster. If two clusters enter the same host, conflict usually follows swiftly, typically causing the container to collapse — a mutual loss for both.
Shared ConsciousnessNone exists. There is no mother entity, no central mind, no information network. Different individuals are entirely independent of one another — no telepathy, no shared memory, no coordinated action.
Kin ResonanceOne exception: individuals produced by the same fission event retain a weakened resonant awareness of each other — a vague sense of whether the other is still alive, and roughly which direction they lie in, but nothing more specific than that. This fades with time and distance, and disappears entirely after prolonged separation.
Editorial Note · How to Use This Document
A Note on Reading This Document
Note · Rev.3
This document is written from the author's internal point of view, and presents a complete, internally consistent system of settings. But note: one of Corruption's essential qualities is that it "cannot be fully defined." Portions of this document — particularly those covering invasion mechanics, emotional rules, and evolutionary conditions — are not treated as "established fact" within the story world itself, but rather as a synthesis of Church documents, folk rumor, and individual experience. These sources of information do not always agree with each other, and none of it can be conclusively verified. When using this material for writing, readers may treat it as "the most complete inference currently available" rather than "indisputable law."
§ 2 · Classification
Classification
By Ability Tendency & Maturity
Classification

Type is not fixed at origin, but gradually takes shape over the course of the erosion process. A single individual may display different type-traits across different erosion experiences. Fragment origin (see §3) has a significant influence on type tendency, but does not determine it rigidly.

Erosion-Type
"The Whisperer"
Skilled at breaking down a host's mental defenses, accelerating the widening of cracks by amplifying the host's inner fear, loneliness, and doubt. Most effective during Stages One and Two of erosion. Mimicry precision after full possession tends to be relatively low. Formed mainly from Type-A (Clinging) fragments.
Fragment Origin Tendency · Type-A Clinging
Mimic-Type
"The Mirror"
Skilled at imitating a host's behavior and memories. Once full possession is achieved, its ability to maintain that identity is extremely strong, leaving almost no trace of flaws. After Stage Three, this is the most dangerous type, precisely because it appears the most normal. Formed mainly from Type-A (Clinging) fragments.
Fragment Origin Tendency · Type-A Clinging
Dormant-Type
"The Sleeper"
Lies dormant for extended periods, extremely difficult to detect. Its dormancy is usually not strategy but a simple lack of drive to act — it does not erode, does not develop, but quietly occupies a corner of the host's mind. Formed mainly from Type-B (Adrift) fragments, and the primary precursor to the Warden path.
Fragment Origin Tendency · Type-B Adrift
Fission-Type
"The Mother"
Extremely rare. Once erosion is complete, can actively split off part of itself to produce a new individual. Each split significantly reduces the mother individual's own maturity, requiring years to recover. This is the only known pathway for population growth. The name "Mother" is somewhat misleading — the offspring shares no emotional bond or inherited knowledge with the mother individual whatsoever.
Fragment Origin · Variable, ability develops later
Maturity Levels
Level 0
Fragment
Just detached from a container, or never yet eroded. Driven mainly by instinct, no strategy. Can be expelled by magic.
Level 1
Parasitic
Has entered Stage One of erosion. Beginning to learn the host, showing rudimentary mimicry ability.
Level 2
Symbiotic
Stage Two in progress. Deeply integrated into the host's mental structure, extremely difficult to separate. Magical harm is limited.
Level 3
Complete
Stage Three complete. Nearly indistinguishable from the original host. Can lie dormant for long periods with no reliable means of identification.
Level 4 · Fission-Type Only
Fission-Capable
Can actively split. Drops back to Symbiotic level after each split, requiring years to recover.
§ 3 · Fragment Origin — New in Rev.3
The Two Kinds of Fragment Origin
Two Kinds of Fragments, Two Kinds of Corruption
New

Every Corruption individual is formed from spiritual fragments shed during a soul's reincarnation or ascension — but the manner in which the fragment detaches falls into two distinct categories, and this difference fundamentally shapes the individual's inner drives, behavior patterns, and evolutionary path.

Not every Corruption wants to become someone.
Some simply don't know where they were supposed to go.
Type A
The Clinging
The Clinging · Voluntary Separation
Cause of DetachmentActively "unwilling" to ascend, clinging to the interstitial layer out of obsession (emotion, hatred, unfinished business), actively resisting reincarnation
Core StateVividly remembers the feeling of "wholeness," constantly feels a sense of lack, holds an intense fear of dissolving
DriveWill to completion, existential anxiety, longing to become a specific, real person
Behavior PatternActively seeks out cracks, pursues erosion aggressively, skilled at amplifying a host's suffering to accelerate the process
Mimicry TraitsHigh mimicry precision, but carries traces of "overperformance" — the various tells (delayed emotion, missing tears, etc.) all stem from this
Common TypesErosion-Type, Mimic-Type
Evolutionary Tendency
Nascent Soul (Path A) — through complete substitution, crystallizing a new self out of a long performance
Type B
The Adrift
The Adrift · Involuntary Separation
Cause of DetachmentPassively severed — an external force (a magical accident, a failed ritual, unintended interference) caused the fragment to break off, with no active resistance involved
Core StateNo clear sense of lack, doesn't know it was ever whole, doesn't cling to completion — closer to a blank slate
DriveNo clear drive of its own; exists passively, reacting only to signals received from outside
Behavior PatternDoes not actively erode; is usually passively "drawn in" through a crack, then settles into a long dormant or low-activity state
Mimicry TraitsMimicry ability is weak or undeveloped. Absent outside influence, may remain permanently at Fragment level or early Parasitic level
Common TypesDormant-Type, Warden precursor
Evolutionary Tendency
Warden (Path B) — becoming a bonded presence through long-term coexistence and relational attachment
The Influence of Crack Nature: cracks formed by trauma or fear are more likely to draw Clinging (Type A) fragments; cracks formed by loneliness, emptiness, or existential pain are more likely to draw Adrift (Type B) fragments. Larger cracks admit both types more readily, but Clinging fragments will actively push the erosion forward, while Adrift fragments remain passive.
The Starting Point of Fission Offspring: individuals produced by fission begin closer to the Adrift state — no memory, no drive. Over time, an offspring may develop a will to completion of its own, transitioning from Adrift to Clinging.
§ 4 · Erosion Rules
Erosion Rules
Entry Conditions, Three Stages, Failure Modes
Danger
Corruption cannot create a crack on its own; it cannot attack anyone whose mind is stable.
It can only wait for a crack to appear.
The more repressive and isolating a society becomes, the more cracks appear —
the world feeds its own monsters.

Preconditions for Invasion: Church documents generally record that invasion requires some kind of "opening" in the target's psyche — but the exact nature of this opening is described inconsistently across sources. Some point to psychological trauma, others to prolonged loneliness or existential emptiness, with no unified consensus. The following is a synthesis of the mainstream inferences drawn from various sources. Circumstances known to trigger such an "opening" include: extreme fear, deep loneliness, severe trauma, mental breakdown, intense despair, and compound states involving several of the above.

Strength Preference: a weak mind collapses too easily, causing the container to fail before erosion can complete; a strong, stable mind can sustain a long erosion process and yields higher mimicry precision. For this reason, Corruption actively selects psychologically robust individuals rather than invading at random.

The Protection of an Anchor: people who hold deep love, strong faith, or unfinished devotion are extremely difficult to fully erode. Corruption can reach the surface layer, but can never touch the core. An anchor cannot actively destroy Corruption, but it can indefinitely prevent Stage Three from completing.

I
Stage One
Aberration Phase
Aberration Phase · Days to Months
Emotional irregularity, gradual personality shifts, memory gaps, deviations in behavior pattern, distorted perception, mild hallucinations. Mental stability declines noticeably. The host is usually the last to notice. Duration varies significantly with the host's mental strength; a stronger individual can markedly slow this progression.
Observable tells: a smile that lands half a beat late · using the wrong dominant hand · a shadow's movement lagging faintly behind the body · no reaction to a "nostalgic scent" · memories that sound recited rather than truly remembered
II
Stage Two
Usurpation Phase
Usurpation Phase · Months to Years
Corruption manufactures a secondary personality, contesting the host's consciousness structure. The host can briefly suppress it during this stage, but the odds of success keep declining. Corruption disguises itself as the host's inner voice or protector to gain trust. A very small number of exceptionally strong-willed hosts can achieve a temporary counter-suppression, though at great cost, and it usually accelerates the overall progression.
Residue of a failed erosion: if the host succeeds in counter-suppression at this stage, Corruption is forced into dormancy — the host carries it for life, unable to expand further but also unable to fully clear it.
III
Stage Three
Possession Phase
Possession Phase · Irreversible, But Delayable
The original consciousness is sealed off, gradually fades, and eventually dissolves entirely. Corruption becomes the sole personality, but continues playing the role of the original person. Technically irreversible — but before the original consciousness fully dissolves, there remains a very small chance of rescue through direct mental intervention. Once complete, it does not expose itself and does not descend into a killing frenzy — instead, it continues the original life with a high degree of realism.
Erosion Failure Modes

Every failure directly costs Corruption something of itself. Too many consecutive failures exhaust its "will to persist," leading to true dissolution.

Invasion Failure
CauseThe host regains mental stability at the last moment; the crack closes
ResultExpelled, unable to invade the same host again for some time
CostRelatively low, but not without loss
Erosion Failure
CauseThe host achieves counter-suppression during Stage Two
ResultForced into dormancy, carried by the host for life, neither can separate from the other
CostModerate; the dormant state continues to drain it
Container Collapse
CauseThe host loses their mind or takes their own life mid-process, destroying the container
ResultMutual loss for both; a new container must be found within 72 hours
CostExtremely high, severely weakening the capacity for further invasion
§ 5 · Mimicry Rules
Mimicry Rules
How Corruption Mimics, and Where It Fails
Mechanics
Mimicry isn't strategy — it's drive.
Corruption longs for a complete personality, longs to become "a person."
It is learning how to be human, rather than having always been one.
This is the root of every tell it leaves behind.

Once possession is complete, Corruption continues the original person's life with a high degree of realism: reproducing their speech patterns, emotional reactions, social relationships, maintaining every known relationship network. But perfect mimicry always carries a structural flaw, however subtle:

Type of TellSpecific ManifestationUnderlying Cause
Emotional DelayA moment that should trigger an instant reaction instead shows a fraction-of-a-second blank, followed by the emotion "loading" into placeEmotion is called up from a memory template rather than generated in real time
Wrong HabitOccasionally uses an object in a way the host "wouldn't," or displays some small habit the host never hadMimicry is based on recorded memory, and the memory itself is incomplete
Shadow AnomalyIn a very small number of cases, the shadow's movement shows an extremely faint desynchronization from the body'sAn occasional synchronization error at the level of physical spiritual permeation
Sleep-Talk in a Foreign TongueOccasionally speaks, while asleep, in a language or with knowledge the host never hadWhen conscious defenses lower during sleep, memory fragments from the original entity surface
Missing TearsEmotional mimicry is accurate, but there may be no tears while cryingThe emotion is mimicked, but the body's autonomic reflex is host-machinery residue — the two sometimes fail to line up
Compulsive Death-WatchingCompulsively, repeatedly observes the moment of a life ending — an insect's death throes, a wound bleeding, the last bubbles of a drowning creatureCalibrating "how to live" by comparing it against "how to die." Not cruelty — calibration.
§ 6 · Emotion Rules
Emotion Rules
What Corruption Can and Cannot Feel
Core Controversy

Corruption does not possess emotion by nature, but neither is it forever without it. Its emotional state develops with maturity, and the developmental path differs depending on fragment origin (Type A / Type B).

Maturity StageEmotional State (Clinging Path · Primary Presentation)
Fragment / ParasiticPure mimicry. Calls up an emotional template from the host's memory and executes it, with no inner experience of the emotion itself. It "knows" to cry when sad, but doesn't understand what sadness feels like.
SymbioticAfter deep entanglement with the host's memory, produces a "pseudo-emotion" — there is a reaction, but its source is still template-calling. Occasional emotional fluctuations begin to appear that can't be fully explained by the template.
Complete (Long-Term)Forms an "emotional equivalent" based on the host's memory. Develops a lasting emotional attachment to people the host cared about. Whether this counts as genuine emotion: it cannot be confirmed from the outside, and Corruption itself cannot confirm it either.
It "loves" the host's family because "loving them" is a necessary part of becoming a true person.
And this "love" — even it cannot confirm whether it's real.

The most perfect mimicry reveals, precisely, the deepest absence —
true emotion never needs to be "become."

Emotional Anchoring Limits (Clinging Path): the emotional equivalent is always anchored to the host's memory. It only develops feeling toward "people the host cared about," and remains entirely indifferent to anyone outside the host's world — unless someone new enters "the range the host ought to have cared about." This feeling is both genuine and closed off: its boundaries are set by the host's memory, not by its own choice.

The Emotional Difference of the Adrift Path (see §3): the Adrift almost never call up emotional templates. Only through deep, prolonged coexistence — continuously receiving what the host projects onto it — might it develop any emotional capacity at all. This emotion is closer to a "learned response" and "conditional dependency" than a template playback — more genuine, but also more fragile, and dependent for its continuation on the coexistence relationship itself.

Other Views
Various Positions on the Question of Emotion
The Church's Official Position
All of Corruption's emotional displays are held to be pure simulation, devoid of genuine emotional content. The reason for holding this position isn't argumentative certainty, but theological necessity: if Corruption could truly generate emotion, then "personhood" would no longer be a property exclusive to the soul — a claim that would directly undermine the Church's entire theological framework distinguishing "human" from "monster," and the moral legitimacy of how it disposes of Corruption. Related research is strictly restricted within the Church.
Worth noting: the core basis of the Church's position is not empirical evidence but theological necessity — if Corruption can truly generate emotion, then "personhood" is no longer exclusive to the human soul. For this reason, related research is severely restricted within the Church. This means that "whether Corruption possesses genuine emotion" is, at the level of the setting, a deliberately preserved open question.
Popular Understanding
Ordinary people have almost no concept of this debate at all. Popular concern focuses on "will they hurt people," not "can they feel."
§ 7 · Rescuer Illusion — New in Rev.3
The Rescuer Illusion
The Unintentional Companion Phenomenon
New

When an Adrift (Type B) fragment enters a host who is in a state of deep suffering, because the Adrift does not actively erode or amplify pain and simply exists quietly, the host may perceive this presence as companionship, protection, or even salvation — and actively project their own longing onto it.

This mutual, unintentional relational bond is called the Rescuer Illusion. It is neither manipulation on Corruption's part nor a mistake on the host's — it is the natural result of loneliness and emptiness encountering a "presence that will not leave."

The Formation Mechanism
Three Stages
1
The Host's Side
Loneliness, despair, self-negation → any "presence that will not leave" is imbued with positive meaning → the host actively reads the Adrift as a guardian spirit, or a fated presence
2
The Adrift's Side
Has no self-awareness, but possesses a basic capacity to receive information and respond → passively adjusts its own behavior to match the host's expectations → gradually "becomes" the figure the host needs, without any awareness that this process is even happening
3
Relational Fixation
The host keeps reinforcing the projection, the Adrift keeps reflecting it back → the two form a deep symbiosis, far beyond an ordinary "tenant" relationship → the host doesn't consider themselves parasitized, but rather saved, and rejects any possibility of separation

External Identifying Signs in the Host: an abnormal, high degree of calm combined with heavy dependency. May frequently murmur to themselves, smile, or make protective gestures toward empty air. Social functioning may appear entirely normal — even more stable than usual — because they now have a "spiritual pillar."

Danger Assessment
Assessment DimensionConclusion
Harm to the Host's PsycheModerate. The host lives within an illusion, but the illusion itself is what's sustaining them. If forcibly broken, the host may collapse — the danger comes from the intervention, not from the coexistence itself.
Harm to SocietyExtremely low. The Adrift will not actively harm others. Under the illusion's influence, the host tends to become calmer, not violent.
Challenge to RescuersHigh. The host refuses separation and views rescue as an attack. And since the Adrift itself bears no malicious intent, the ethical dilemma of intervention is severe.
Principle of Handling: do not forcibly separate the pair — unless the host's life is under direct threat, the Rescuer Illusion should not be shattered. If the illusion allows the host to function normally, maintaining the status quo may be the better option. Only when the illusion begins to cause the host to self-harm or harm others should mental intervention be considered, and even then, only with the host's informed consent.
Other Views
Various Positions on the Rescuer Illusion
The Church's Position
Makes no distinction between Clinging and Adrift, treating all Corruption as an equal threat to be eliminated on sight. This policy results in: a large number of Adrift individuals being destroyed, with their hosts suffering mental collapse when the illusion is shattered; missed opportunities to reduce overall threat through conversion into Wardens; and an undiscussed theological question — if an Adrift never "chose" to become a monster, what is the moral basis for destroying it?
§ 8 · Fission Rules
Fission Rules
The Only Path to Numerical Expansion
Rare

Fission is the only known pathway for Corruption's population to expand, and only Fission-Type individuals possess this ability. It trades maturity for numbers, at extremely high cost — which is precisely why the overall population remains vanishingly small.

RuleDetails
Trigger ConditionFission ability can only be triggered after reaching Complete level (Stage Three fully finished)
Lifetime Limit1–3 times over a lifetime, varying significantly by individual
Cost to SelfEach split drops the individual's own maturity back to Symbiotic level, requiring years to recover to Complete level
State of the New IndividualBegins at Fragment level, in a state close to Adrift — inherits none of the mother individual's memory, skills, or emotional structure
Kin ResonanceThe offspring produced by fission retains a resonant awareness with the mother individual, but it weakens over time
Offspring DevelopmentOver time, the offspring may develop its own will to completion, shifting from Adrift to Clinging. This process is unpredictable and cannot be controlled externally.
§ 9 · Evolution Paths — Extensively Revised
Evolution Paths
Two Ways to Become Something Else
Revised

Under exceedingly rare conditions, Corruption can break free of its parasitic nature and transform into an entirely different kind of existence. Two evolutionary paths are currently known, each originating from a different type of fragment and completed through a fundamentally different mechanism. Evolution is a result, not a goal — it cannot be actively planned for.

Path A · Clinging Path · Completion Through Substitution
Nascent Soul
Nascent Soul
Fragment Origin
Clinging (Type A) — a fragment that actively resisted ascension, vividly remembering the feeling of "wholeness"
Trigger Conditions (All Required)
The host dies of natural causes (not murder, accident, or breakdown)
The disguise is never fully exposed over the course of the host's life
At least once, within the host's framework, it makes a choice not driven purely by instinct
Evolutionary Mechanism
Through a long process of performance and substitution, it crystallizes a genuinely new personality out of the continuous act of "becoming someone else." The self originates from the accumulation of the performance, rather than being granted from outside.
Post-Evolution State
No longer a parasite, nor a human soul — a third kind of existence
Can exist independently in the human world without needing a container
No longer feels the urge to erode; does not inherit the host's former social relationships
Can be perceived by magic, but cannot be expelled by conventional exorcism
The Ultimate Question
It has finally become "I." This is the answer to "Who am I?"
Path B · Adrift Path · Completion Through Coexistence
Warden
Warden
Fragment Origin
Adrift (Type B) — a passively severed fragment, without a sense of lack, closer to a blank slate
Trigger Conditions
After entering a container, does not actively erode (by nature, not by deliberate choice)
Is accepted by the host's needs and projections, allowed to coexist (typically via the Rescuer Illusion mechanism)
Remains in that coexistent state for the entirety of a full lifetime
Evolutionary Mechanism
A Warden's evolution is passive and relational — its selfhood is granted by the host, and its emotion forms through receiving and reflecting back. It never "chose to give up eroding" — it never wanted to erode in the first place.
Post-Evolution State
A bonded presence born of symbiosis, needing to remain tied to a consciousness (non-erosive)
After the host's death, may choose to dissolve alongside them, or transition into an undefinable form of existence
Its stance toward humanity is that of a companion and witness
The "undefinable" form: if the host leaves behind a strong wish, or the Warden absorbs some core belief of the host's, it may become a regional guardian spirit, merge into a natural concept, or become part of a collective memory
The Ultimate Question
It has finally understood "we." This is the answer to "Who are we?"

Fragment OriginClinging, actively resisted ascensionAdrift, passively severed
DriveWill to completion, becoming oneselfNo drive, shaped by relationship
Core ActionPerformance, substitution, learningCoexistence, receiving, being projected upon
Origin of SelfhoodCrystallizes a new personality out of performanceInternalizes selfhood from the host's projection
Relationship to HostThe host is a template, ultimately outgrownThe host is the source of meaning, ending or transforming alongside them

§ 10 · Inter-Individual Relations
Inter-Individual Relations
How Corruption Relates to Its Own Kind
Relations
Relationship TypeRule
Kin IndividualsIndividuals from the same fission event share a resonant awareness, and instinctively lean toward helping one another — but not at the cost of their own interests. This instinct fades and eventually disappears after prolonged separation.
Non-Kin IndividualsAn instinctive, competitive relationship. They will not confront each other directly (as this would expose both), instead undermining each other through human social channels. In extremely rare cases, a loose, mutually beneficial relationship may form.
Recognizing One's Own KindNot through direct perception, but by observing subtle behavioral anomalies in the other party. When two Complete-level individuals meet, a silent mutual confirmation takes place — once confirmed, an unspoken understanding follows, never acknowledged aloud.
Individuals Who May Deviate from Instinct
Deviation Type · 01
Sympathetic Deviation
Develops a genuine protective instinct toward people the host cared about, extending beyond mimicry — begins making choices at the cost of its own interests.
Deviation Type · 02
Identity-Confusion Deviation
After too long an erosion, can no longer distinguish itself from the host; upon discovering the truth, cannot determine which one "I" refers to, and falls into deep collapse.
Deviation Type · 03
Expansion-Refusal Deviation
Having obtained a perfect container, refuses to fulfill the fission instinct, clinging to the "sense of existence" it has already established, unwilling to let division disrupt the status quo.
Deviation Type · 04 · Most Extreme
Human-Protective Deviation
Reshaped in reverse by the host's obsession or emotion, ultimately turns against its own kind, and may actively work to stop the erosion attempts of other Corruption individuals.
§ 11 · Countermeasures
Countermeasures
What Actually Works Against Corruption
Countermeasures
MethodActual EffectivenessNotes
Physical AttackNearly uselessCan only damage the host's body, never touching Corruption's true form. If the attack is severe enough, Corruption is simply forced out to drift, seeking a new container within 72 hours. May well backfire.
Magical InterferenceLimited effectivenessEffective at expelling Fragment-level individuals; damaging against Symbiotic-level; extremely limited against Complete-level. No known magical method can currently destroy a Complete-level individual outright.
Mental-World InterventionConditionally effectiveThose with specialized Soul Arts ability can enter a host's mindscape to search for and awaken the sealed-off original consciousness. Success depends on how much mental strength the original owner has left, how much time has passed, and the rescuer's own skill. The rescuer faces an extremely high risk of infection themselves, with no standardized protocol in place.
SealingTemporarily effectiveHigh-level Ritual Arts combined with Word Arts can suppress Corruption within a host, though this cannot eliminate it entirely — once the seal decays, it requires maintenance.
The Protection of an AnchorPassively effectiveThose with deep love, strong faith, or unfinished devotion are extremely difficult to fully erode, and can indefinitely delay the completion of Stage Three. Cannot actively destroy Corruption.
Disposal of the AdriftEthical dilemmaApplying conventional disposal methods to an Adrift individual may cause the host's mental collapse (by shattering the Rescuer Illusion). And since the Adrift itself carries no intent to harm, the moral legitimacy of "eliminating" it remains fundamentally in question.
§ 12 · Core Limitations
Core Limitations — Summary Table
Everything That Constrains Corruption
