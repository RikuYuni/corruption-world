Riku Yuni's public persona is a single, unified one — a quiet, professional restorer who doesn't let people get close easily. Most of his clients meet him only once: they collect the object he's fixed, leave behind a modest sum, and never make contact again.

He's usually called the "Seal-Smith" or "the Silent Monk of Objects." Only a handful of regulars know his name is Riku Yuni — and none of them know what that name actually means on the inside.

Writing note: the outside world only ever sees "Riku Yuni" as a single whole. The two personas' individual names are internal worldbuilding details and generally shouldn't come up in the narrative, except in scenes involving their internal dialogue or a shared mindscape.
§ 2 · Persona — Yuni
Yuni · Feminine Persona
Chapter Two
Yuni
The Feminine Persona — Observer, Analyst

Of the two personas, Yuni is the one more oriented toward perception. She isn't necessarily "quieter," but she's more inclined to observe and understand first, and only then decide whether it's worth speaking.

§ 2.1 · Core Personality
Core Personality

· Observer's instinct — She's in the habit of watching a thing through to the end before speaking. This isn't hesitation; it's a natural aversion to "incomplete judgment."

· Precise but restrained — She talks less than Riku, but every time she speaks, it carries weight. She doesn't waste words, and occasionally a single sentence from her will leave the other person quiet for a long time.

· Emotionally reserved — She has feelings, but rarely expresses them outright. She tends to respond through action or silence rather than words.

· More attuned to sealed objects — When handling sealed objects, Yuni is more likely to pick up on the "emotional layer" within the resonance — especially signals of sorrow or obsession.

She isn't cold. She simply keeps most of what she feels on the inside, and doesn't see the need for anyone else to know.
§ 2.2 · Sensory Experience
Sensory Traits

When Yuni is in the lead perceptually, her intake of the outside world is closer to "full-spectrum reception" — strong at picking up detail, but also more prone to discomfort in noisy environments.

With sealed-object resonance, she can sense a layer that Riku cannot — not just knowing that an object carries a resonance, but actually feeling the "texture" of the emotion, the temperature it had at the time.

This ability makes her extremely valuable in restoration work, but it's also one of the reasons she tires more easily.
Sample Scene · The Atmosphere When Yuni Leads

She rested her hand on the ring and didn't move. A long silence passed.
Riku waited behind her, without asking.
"It's looking for a place," she said at last. "Not anywhere specific. Just… somewhere to stop."
Riku was quiet for a moment. "Can we help it, then?"
"Maybe. Let's take a look first."

§ 3 · Persona — Riku
Riku · Masculine Persona
Chapter Three
Riku
The Masculine Persona — Executor, Analyst

Of the two personas, Riku is the one more oriented toward action. He isn't reckless, but he's more inclined to "get started and figure it out as he goes" rather than wait until he fully understands before moving.

§ 3.1 · Core Personality
Core Personality

· Executor's instinct — Faced with a problem, he tends to build "just enough understanding" and then start working. He won't just sit there because he hasn't fully thought it through yet.

· Direct — He talks more than Yuni, and more bluntly. Sometimes he's already speaking while Yuni is still observing. She'll occasionally rein him in gently, internally.

· More outwardly curious — He has a strong drive to understand "how exactly this thing works." Part of why he studies sealed objects is simply that he finds it fun.

· More structurally attuned to sealed objects — When he handles sealed objects, he's more likely to read the "incantation layer" and "structural layer," which suits him well for judging a sealed object's mechanism and danger level.

Riku isn't reckless — he's just more willing to accept the logic of "step in first, ask questions later." That's an advantage in restoration work, but occasionally a risk when facing a dangerous sealed object.
§ 3.2 · Sensory Experience
Sensory Traits

When Riku is in the lead perceptually, his intake of the outside world is more directional — he instinctively filters out "irrelevant" information and focuses on the core of whatever task is at hand.

This makes him more efficient when handling complex sealed objects, but it also means he sometimes overlooks side details — and those side details are occasionally exactly what Yuni catches.

Sample Scene · The Atmosphere When Riku Leads

He flipped the sealed object over and ran a finger along the edge.
"Here," he said. "This bit of incantation was added later — and whoever added it wasn't paying attention."
From behind, Yuni asked: what happened to that person?
"No idea. Doesn't matter — let's strip this part out first."
Yuni said nothing more, but she made a note of it.

§ 4 · Dual Persona Mechanics
Shared by Both Personas
Chapter Four
Dual-Persona Mechanics
How Yuni and Riku Coexist

Yuni and Riku don't coexist by taking turns with the body — theirs is a genuine, parallel existence. They can be online at the same time, switch which of them leads freely, and even during an "incomplete switch," each can independently handle a different channel of perception or behavior.

But parallel existence doesn't mean they're always in step. On core judgment calls — whether to take on a dangerous commission, whether to trust someone, whether to touch a particular sealed object — the two personas can disagree sharply. This isn't friction from complementary personalities; it's real opposition, and sometimes it can't be quickly resolved.

External signs of conflict: when disagreement crosses a certain threshold, something like "left hand fighting right hand" can occur — one hand doing something, the other doing the opposite; walking up to a door and stopping, then repeating the approach twice; switching tone mid-sentence, as if two people were taking turns speaking. This isn't the norm, but it can happen under high pressure.

"Ceding" control of the body: in situations that call for quick action or a direct decision, Yuni will often "temporarily hand the body over" to Riku — she recedes to the perceptual layer, stops issuing action commands, and lets Riku take full control. From the outside, Riku Yuni will seem to "change": speaking faster, acting more directly, hesitating less. In situations that call for delicate perception, Riku will likewise cede control to Yuni.

Riku's more outward-facing temperament: Riku's way of speaking is more direct and casual than Yuni's. He'll talk to himself in the workshop, addressing his tools or a sealed object; he walks faster on the street and looks straight ahead; he'll occasionally strike up a conversation with a stranger (while Yuni rolls her eyes internally). He has a dry sense of humor — not meant to make anyone else laugh, but said because he found it funny himself.

§ 4.1 · Fatigue Dynamics
Fatigue Mechanics

The dual-persona structure has a special compensatory ability in terms of stamina: when one persona enters a fatigued state, the other can take over and maintain the capacity to act. Under normal circumstances the two personas don't compete for control of the body, but conflict remains a real possibility — under high pressure or high-stakes disagreement, this compensatory mechanism can actually increase the tension between them.

However, this is not true "rest." The persona taking over is also expending mental resources; the overall fatigue keeps accumulating — its perception is simply delayed.

The result is: Riku Yuni can sustain a longer stretch of action within a single event than an ordinary person, but the overall fatigue debt is harder to notice and harder to settle in time.

He sometimes becomes suddenly, extremely exhausted with little visible warning, which can confuse the people around him. He himself has grown used to it, but still can't always accurately predict when it will hit.
§ 4.2 · External Presentation
How He Presents Externally

Because the two personas run in parallel so frequently, Riku Yuni carries, to outside observers, a hard-to-define layered quality: sometimes his way of speaking is restrained and precise; sometimes he suddenly becomes more direct and driven; and sometimes both styles surface within the same sentence.

People unfamiliar with dual-persona phenomena usually don't realize what this is — they just feel this person is "a bit strange," or "feels like two people," without being able to pin down exactly what's off.

§ 6 · Corruption Resonance
Core Setting · Author's Internal Note
Chapter Six
Corruption Resonance
The Resonance Within — Origin & Implications
Origin Incident
Author's Internal Setting

As a child, out of intense curiosity about sealed objects, Riku Yuni accidentally came into contact with a sealed object whose grade far exceeded what he could handle at the time. That object carried a Corruption trace — not a complete Corruption individual, but a fragment-level residue of consciousness left behind after some past erosion event.

During the contact, part of the residue's structure seeped into Riku Yuni's mental layer, forming a permanent Corruption resonance. This is not being eroded by Corruption, nor is it a precursor to becoming a host — it's something closer to a "mark." The resonance exists within his mental structure, but has no consciousness, no initiative of its own, and will not actively expand.

The incident was never recorded by the Church (no one was present at the time), and it has never been systematically diagnosed. Riku Yuni himself is aware the resonance exists, but doesn't fully understand its exact nature or origin.

§ 6.1 · Effect on Corruption Perception
Effect on Corruption's Ability to Identify Him

Because of the Corruption resonance he carries, Riku Yuni's mental structure gives off a particular signal to Corruption — something like "resonating at the same frequency."

To an actual Corruption individual, this signal is exceedingly rare, and it causes confusion in identification: it cannot immediately determine whether Riku Yuni is human, or some special kind of its own kind.

This is why a certain Corruption individual has developed a particular fixation on Riku Yuni — not because it has marked him as prey, but because it's confused. That signal keeps drawing it closer, trying to work out exactly what this being is.

This is currently the only known case of "a Corruption individual actively directing sustained attention at a specific human," and the root cause isn't that Riku Yuni has some special value — it's that the resonance inside him is throwing off Corruption's identification mechanism.
§ 6.2 · Effect on Riku Yuni's Abilities
Effect on His Own Abilities

The resonance's presence gives Riku Yuni's Soul Arts perception a boost in certain specific situations — especially when handling sealed objects connected to Corruption. He can read information layers that an ordinary restorer simply cannot reach.

At the same time, though, this also means his mental structure is more open to interference from Corruption, and less defended against it. This is a risk he is only vaguely, not fully, aware of.

§ 6.3 · What Riku Yuni Knows
How Much He Himself Understands

Riku Yuni knows there is "something abnormal" inside him — he can feel it resonate when he handles certain sealed objects. But he has never been able to accurately identify what it is.

He tends to understand it as "some kind of perceptual quirk left over from a childhood accident," rather than "a Corruption resonance," because he has never had a complete framework of knowledge that would let him make that diagnosis.

His attitude toward it is closer to "something he's grown used to living with" than "a problem to be solved" — it's always been there, and it hasn't turned him into anything else, so he's chosen to coexist with it.

This "having grown used to it" is itself a potential point of narrative tension: what will he think, the day he truly understands what this thing is?

Behavioral Details
How He Acts in the World

The following are behavior patterns for writing reference — not personality labels, but details of what he'd actually do day to day.

Crowds & Social Contact
Actively avoids dense contact
Dual perception means he bears a higher sensory load in crowds. He walks along the edges, unconsciously speeds up in crowded places, and dislikes being approached from behind. It isn't social anxiety — it's an instinctive avoidance of sensory overload.
Physical Contact
Instinctive flinching
He has an instinctive flinch response to sudden physical contact. His sense of pain runs stronger than it appears — when hurt, he feels more pain than he shows, but he's usually already learned to manage that reaction.
Work Mode
Extremely focused, hard to interrupt
Once he's in work mode, the outside world all but disappears for him. Calling his name may take three tries. He's not ignoring anyone — he's simply completely inside it.
Attitude Toward Sealed Objects
Listen first, act after
On encountering a new sealed object, he'll usually do nothing at first, just let himself sense it for a while — sometimes several minutes. This isn't hesitation; he's "listening."
Signs of Fatigue
Quiet and still
When deeply exhausted, he'll sit down wherever's nearest and do nothing, just stare into space. It doesn't necessarily look severe, but internally, both personas have gone quiet. It's the closest he gets to "powering down."
Speech Habits
Says little, but accurately
He doesn't waste words, and doesn't explain every judgment he makes. If he says "there's something wrong with this," there usually is — even if, at the time, he can't say exactly where the problem lies. His instinctive read can be trusted.
Curiosity
The stranger, the more interested
He has a personal interest in "unusual things" that goes beyond professional necessity. The harder a sealed object is to explain, the more he wants to understand it. This trait is more visible in Riku, but Yuni shares it too, just expressed more quietly.
Home Life
A homebody, but not disorganized
The workshop is kept functionally tidy — every tool has a fixed place, because disorder would get in the way of work. The living area on the ground floor is a different story: not deliberately arranged, but not quite "messy" either — just "not something he pays much mind to."
§ 8 · Inner State
Inner State
Chapter Eight
Inner State
What It's Like to Be Riku Yuni
His pain is not the main theme.
His daily life is not a constant struggle.
He simply lives in a very particular way,
and has, by and large, come to accept it.
— Character Tone Note
§ 8.1 · Relationship with the Dual State
His Relationship with the Dual-Persona State

There's no ongoing conflict between Yuni and Riku, but on key judgment calls, real disagreement can arise. They are two genuinely distinct persons, each with their own preferences and judgments; on core questions they can sometimes find themselves in real opposition, not just a difference of opinion.

They typically resolve disagreements through internal dialogue — quick, brief, and sometimes without needing a conclusion at all, simply each knowing what the other thinks. Most of the time they find an approach both can accept. When they can't, they can't. Silence is also a way of dealing with it.

This coexistent state is, to Riku Yuni, simply "normal." He has never lived any other way, so he has no frame of reference to call it "abnormal." It's just his mode of existence — friction and the occasional standoff included.

The one thing that occasionally gives him pause is this: when the outside world describes "a person," it defaults to that person being singular. He isn't always sure that default applies to him.
§ 8.2 · Relationship with Anomaly
His Relationship with "Anomaly"

Riku Yuni has been around sealed objects since childhood, sensing things "outside the normal framework" from an early age. In his world, "anomaly" isn't a terrifying exception — it's simply another kind of normal.

This means that, faced with something strange, his first reaction usually isn't fear or rejection, but curiosity, followed by a desire to understand. He can't simply walk away, indifferent, from something odd — he'll want to know what it is, and why it's the way it is.

This curiosity is the real reason he became a restorer — not just for the money.

§ 8.3 · Core Emotional Texture
Core Emotional Texture

· He isn't happy, but he isn't suffering, either. His baseline emotional state is closer to "steady calm" — occasionally moved by something, but not easily trapped for long.

· He's wary, but doesn't distrust everyone. He just needs more time to build trust, because he can perceive more of "the person" than most people can — sometimes even the layer someone hasn't put into words.

· He has a particular sensitivity to "endings." Restoring sealed objects is, at its core, work spent handling things that "couldn't be let go of" — he has a deeper understanding of this than most people, which is why he sometimes goes quiet for a long time after finishing a restoration. Not out of sadness — just taking in the weight of it.

He doesn't need to be understood, but every so often, he'd like someone nearby who says nothing, and just is.
§ 8.4 · Relationship with Loneliness
His Relationship with Loneliness

Riku Yuni has had two personas existing side by side since birth, and has never truly experienced being "alone." Because of this, his understanding of "loneliness" has always remained on the level of knowledge, never of felt experience.

· He cannot understand loneliness as a lived emotion. When someone says "I feel so lonely," he can, on a knowledge level, understand what the word means — but he can't resonate with it on a felt level. He knows it's supposed to be a kind of suffering, but to him it's like a color described only in theory — namable, but never seen.

· He once tried "pretending to be lonely." He deliberately kept Riku from speaking, tried to act as if Riku didn't exist, attempting to enter a state of "being just one person." Each attempt lasted about three minutes before he gave up — not because he couldn't do it, but because he had no frame of reference to confirm what "being just one person" was supposed to feel like. He never knew what he was "supposed" to feel in those three minutes.

· He holds a spectator's curiosity about "loneliness." What does it actually feel like? Can a person really suffer just from no one else being present? He'll occasionally watch people who are alone — someone sitting on a bench, someone staring out a window — trying to understand it from the outside. He watches carefully, but he also knows this may be something he can never understand, because what he's missing isn't information, but a point of comparison.

Riku has zero interest in the question. His response is: "So there's no one else around. And then what?"
§ 9 · Relations with the World
External Relations
Chapter Nine
Relations with the World
How Riku Yuni Exists in the World Around Him
§ 9.1 · With the Church
With the Church

Riku Yuni doesn't belong to the Church and isn't under its jurisdiction — he's an independent, commission-based restorer operating at the edge of the official system. The Church knows he exists, but has so far chosen to "tolerate" rather than actively manage him, since a fair portion of the sealed objects he handles are of a type the Church would rather not deal with publicly.

His attitude toward the Church is one of deliberate distance: he knows the Church holds a great deal he doesn't know, and he knows it will never tell him. He accepts this reality, but it doesn't mean he trusts the Church.

If the Church ever needed him to do something, he would ask "why" first, rather than simply agreeing.
§ 9.2 · With Clients
With Clients

His relationships with clients are extremely shallow — most people come once, collect what he's fixed, and disappear. He doesn't maintain contact on his own, and most clients wouldn't know how to establish a longer connection with him anyway.

There are occasional exceptions: certain clients, because they've brought him something that genuinely interests him, get asked a few extra questions — which, for him, already counts as a considerable degree of active engagement.

§ 9.3 · With Sealed Objects
With Sealed Objects

This is his truest, deepest, longest-running "relationship." His basement holds years' worth of accumulated sealed objects — some left behind after a commission was completed for further study, some he sought out himself.

His attitude toward sealed objects isn't that of "tools" — it's closer to "things that need to be understood." He'll sit beside a sealed object for hours, doing nothing, just sensing it. This isn't inefficiency — it's his way of understanding them.

Sample Scene · His Everyday Distance from the World

Sometimes, late at night, he goes up to the ground floor and sits by the window, listening to the rain.
The sounds of the city feel far away from him — lights, voices, the noise of the market closing up for the night.
He doesn't feel lonely, and he doesn't feel not-lonely.
He's simply there, both of him, and after a while, he goes back down to keep working.

§ 10 · Open Questions
Open Questions
Chapter Ten
Open Questions
What Remains Unknown — Even to the Author

The following are core narrative threads about Riku Yuni that currently have no definitive answer, kept here as reference for future writing.

Q1
What exactly was that sealed object?
The heart of the childhood incident. The sealed object that left the Corruption resonance inside him — how did it end up in circulation among ordinary people? What is its own origin? Does it still exist? Might he encounter it again someday, and learn more about the resonance from it?
Q2
Will that Corruption ever figure out what he is?
The Corruption individual whose attention was drawn to him by the misidentification — as it keeps approaching him, how will its understanding of Riku Yuni evolve? When it finally realizes he's human rather than one of its own kind, what will it do? Lose interest — or form something more complicated?
Q3
Will the resonance always just be a resonance?
The current setting holds that the resonance has no consciousness, no initiative, and won't expand. But "currently" is the key word. As Riku Yuni continues to come into contact with high-grade sealed objects and Corruption-related relics, might the resonance's state change? This remains an open narrative possibility.
Q4
The origin of the dual personas
The current setting offers no explanation for why Riku Yuni was born with two personas from the start. This can remain a mystery, or be explained partway through the narrative. If there is an explanation, is it connected to the Corruption resonance — or is it a wholly separate matter?
Q5
How would Riku Yuni view Corruption's capacity for emotion?
The sealed objects he handles every day are "the obsessions left behind by souls that couldn't let go." He understands the weight of "wanting to keep existing" better than almost anyone. If he ever truly comes to understand the origin of Corruption and the question of its emotions, where would he stand? This is the fundamental thing that sets him apart from everyone else.
Creative Notes
·None of the above questions need to be fully answered. Preserving the mystery itself is part of the narrative strategy.
·Q5 has the greatest narrative potential of any question here. Riku Yuni's uniqueness lies not in how strong he is, but in how differently he sees things compared to everyone else.
